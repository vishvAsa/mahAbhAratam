ON THE MEANING  
OF  
THE MAHABHARATA  

There on the ethno-psychological plane, from what we are now sure of the work, we can see the pious struggling to get out of the rules of the game[^front_1] in the conduct & ritual obscure, man's chief duty towards his neighbor and his God.  
The four other themes, the epic carves out with firm up the word, feel much for all but the paramountcy of moral values and too many to be born to the will—(1.15. 17.21). dharma be obligatory but with the parting word motto of "Let thy reason be fixed on Dharma, and let thy mind be ever great." The will strives to get yet higher.
