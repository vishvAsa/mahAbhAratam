## ŚLOKA I

It is clearly established in the Śāstra, namely Bhagavadgītā that Lord Nārāyaṇa is the Supreme Brahman,[^68_1] who is to be attained by bhakti alone.[^68_2] And, that bhakti is to be accomplished by the performance of one's own prescribed rites,[^68_3] by the knowledge of the true nature of the self[^68_4] and by non-attachment to worldly objects.[^68_5]

### NOTES:

1. According to Viśiṣṭādvaita, Lord Nārāyaṇa along with his consort Lakṣmī is the Supreme Reality. The Bhagavadgītā, which is an epitome of the Upaniṣads, mentions Him as the Supreme Reality. See also *GS.V.*, p. 2:
   > *tattvaṃ jijñāsamānānāṃ hetubhiḥ sarvatomukhaiḥ /‌*  
   > *tattvam eko mahāyogī harirnārāyaṇātparaḥ //‌*

   See p. 2 of the present dissertation: "ataḥ sakalopaniṣat-sāra-saṅkalanātmikāsu bhagavadgītāsvapi para-tattva-bhūta-para-brahmaiva prādhānyena pratipādyam."

[^68_1]: According to Viśiṣṭādvaita, Lord Nārāyaṇa along with his consort Lakṣmī is the Supreme Reality. The Bhagavadgītā, which is an epitome of the Upaniṣads, mentions Him as the Supreme Reality. See also *GS.V.*, p. 2: *tattvaṃ jijñāsamānānāṃ hetubhiḥ sarvatomukhaiḥ / tattvam eko mahāyogī harirnārāyaṇātparaḥ //* See p. 2 of the present dissertation: *ataḥ sakalopaniṣadsārasaṅkalanātmikāsu bhagavadgītāsvapi paratattvabhūtaparabrahmaiva prādhānyena pratipādyam.*

[[P69]]

2. *mahanīyaviṣaye prītiḥ bhaktiḥ*. Bhakti is the sole means of attaining the Supreme, is stated thus: "bhaktaikalabhye puruṣe purāṇe..." See *GS.V*, p. 3.
3. *Svadharmaḥ*. The word is interpreted as *sve dharmaḥ* (wherein the usage is in the locative case). It is also interpreted as *svasya dharmaḥ* (wherein the usage is with the genitive case-ending). By both interpretations, one's own enjoined duties in accordance with one's class and stage of life is intended. According to the commentator, the word *sva* at the beginning also conveys the sense of benediction. *GS.V*, p. 3. Cf. *ubhayathāpi svavarṇāśramadharma ityarthaḥ*, *GS.V*, p. 3.
4. The knowledge of the self means the knowledge of one's own self whose essential nature consists in being subordinate to the Supreme Lord: *jñānam atra pāraśeṣataikarasa-svātma-viṣayaṃ jñānam*, *GS.V*, p. 3.
5. "Non-attachment" (Vairāgya) means a strong dislike [[P70]] towards anything other than the Supreme Lord. Cf. *paramātmani yo rakto virakto'paramātmani*, *GS.V*, p. 3; also cf. GSR, p. 4.

## ŚLOKA II

In the first six chapters the performance of karma-yoga and jñāna-yoga is enjoined[^70_1] for the purpose of the experience of the self.[^70_2] This jñāna-yoga and karma-yoga, when performed with the view to please the Lord, lead to the yoga and through which the enjoyment of the self.[^70_3]

### NOTES:

1. The performance of the daily and occasional rites that are enjoined according to one's own capacity. Cf. *karmayogo nāma devārcanaprabhṛtiṣu daśāsu madhye yathāvagamaṃ yathāśakti yathāruci vā ... nityanaimittika-karmajātam .... nityānuṣṭhānam*, *GS.V*, pp. 6–7.  
   Jñānayoga is stated as: *jñānayogo nāma uktakarmayogena labdhādhikārasya karmayogatvaveṣaṃ parityajya nityanaimittikadharmānuṣṭhānam*, *GS.V*, p. 6.

## ŚLOKA III

In the next six chapters bhakti-yoga is expounded[^71_1] in detail[^71_2] which is accomplished by the performance of the jñāna-yoga and karma-yoga for the purpose of the enjoyment of the Supreme Reality, namely, Bhagavan[^71_3] in His true nature.[^71_4]

### NOTES:

1. By the first six chapters, and by the subsequent six chapters, the definition of karma, jñāna and the bhakti-yogas which are the means, and the nature of [[P72]] the three Realities, namely, sentient, insentient and the Lord are elaborately dealt with (*GS.V.*, p. 9).

2. "Detailed exposition" means the true nature of bhakti along with its accessories and the result achieved by such a bhakti (*GS.V.*, p. 9).
3. The term *bhaga* in the word *bhagavān* is primarily applicable only in the case of Lord Nārāyaṇa, that is only to the one who has the six qualities, namely, *jñāna*, *bala*, *aiśvarya*, *śakti*, *vīrya* and *tejas*, in inexhaustible measure without any imperfection. This word is figuratively applicable in the case of others (*GS.V.*, p. 8). Also see *VP.*, 6-5-77.
4. His true nature means the unlimited form of the Lord as conceived by the mind (*GS.V.*, p. 8).

### NOTES:

2. Both the bound and the released souls are meant here (*GS.V.*, p. 10).

4. Distinction of their essential nature.
5. The last six chapters, which subserve the previous two parts is divided into two halves. The first three deal with the three Realities (the insentient, the sentient and the Lord) and the subsequent three deal with the performance of karma-yoga etc. (*GS.V.*, p. 10).

Thus in the verse, the import of the ślokas commencing from the beginning of the text and ending with *tamuvāca hṛṣīkeśaḥ* etc., (II.10) is summarised (*GS.V.*, p. 12).

## ŚLOKA VIII

### NOTES:

1. The Lord's greatness remains unaffected even though He incarnates in various forms such as matsya, kūrma, etc. Here, explaining the greatness of the Lord is incidental because the Lord instructed this karma-yoga in previous ages such as kṛta, tretā, etc., to Vivasvān, who in turn handed down this instruction traditionally to his descendants, and the same karma-yoga is being instructed by the Lord now. Arjuna has a doubt as to how Lord Kṛṣṇa Himself would have instructed [[P78]] this to those persons. In order to remove this doubt, the Lord explains the true nature of His incarnations. Cf. *Bh.Gītā-bhāṣya*, pp. 262–263. By this, the meaning of the Bhagavad Gītā text (IV.9) is brought out. Also cf. *GS.V*, p. 16.

## ŚLOKA XI

The true nature of His own self (that is, the Supreme Lord),[^82_1] His concealment by the insentient matter,[^82_2] whole-hearted surrender unto Him,[^82_3] distinction among the devotees of Him,[^82_4] and the superiority of those devotees who are devoted to Him (for Himself)[^82_5] are elaborated in the seventh chapter.

### NOTES:

1. The true nature of the Lord is that He is the *śeṣin* (the principal entity) of the sentient and insentient entities. He is the cause of everything, the supporter and controller of all, and is conveyed by all words. Cf. *GS.V.*, p. 19.

[^82_1]: The true nature of the Lord is that He is the *śeṣin* (the principal entity) of the sentient and insentient entities. He is the cause of everything, the supporter and controller of all, and is conveyed by all words. Cf. *GS.V.*, p. 19.

2. It is the qualities, namely, *sattva*, *rajas* and *tamas*, that conceal the true nature of the Lord. Since prakṛti is the substratum of those qualities, it is spoken of as the concealing factor. By the word prakṛti, the meaning of the BG text VII.14 is intended. According to Viśiṣṭādvaita, the word *māyā* conveys prakṛti or the wondrous power of God, and not an indeterminable entity. Cf. *GSR*, p. 52 and *GS.V*, pp. 19–20.

## ŚLOKA XXX

The paramaikāntins attain their existence by meditation upon the Supreme Lord, visiting the holy abodes where He dwells in iconic form, by worshipping Him, by relating His glory to the disciples, by singing His glory, etc.[^106_1] The activities of their vital breath,[^106_2] mind[^106_3] and intellect depend on Him alone.
